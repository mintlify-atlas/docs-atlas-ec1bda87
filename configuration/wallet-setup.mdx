---
title: "Set up a dedicated wallet for Polymarket trading"
description: "How to create a dedicated Polygon wallet, fund it with USDC.e and MATIC, connect it to Polymarket, and locate your proxy wallet address for the bot."
---

The bot requires a dedicated Polygon wallet that is separate from any wallet you use for personal funds. Isolation limits blast radius if a private key is ever compromised and keeps trading activity easy to audit. By the end of this guide you will have a funded wallet, a Polymarket proxy wallet address, and a private RPC endpoint — all three values needed for your `.env` file.

## Create a dedicated trading wallet

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a new wallet">
    Open MetaMask (or any EVM-compatible wallet) and create a **brand new account** — do not reuse an existing one. Give it a recognizable name such as "Polymarket Bot".
  </Step>
  <Step title="Switch to Polygon Mainnet">
    Add or select the **Polygon Mainnet** network (Chain ID: `137`, RPC: `https://polygon-rpc.com`). All Polymarket activity runs on Polygon.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Export the private key">
    In MetaMask: **Settings → Security & Privacy → Export private key**. Enter your password when prompted. Copy the key — it starts with `0x` and is 66 characters long.

    Set this value as `PRIVATE_KEY` in your `.env`.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Never use your main personal wallet for this bot. Never share your private key with anyone, paste it into a website, or commit it to version control. Anyone with the private key has full control of the associated funds.
</Warning>

## Fund your wallet

The bot trades USDC on Polygon. You need two tokens:

**USDC.e (bridged USDC)**

Polymarket uses USDC.e — the bridged version of USDC on Polygon — not native USDC. The contract address is `0x2791Bca1f2de4661ED88A30C99A7a9449Aa84174`. Make sure any exchange or bridge you use sends to this contract.

You can acquire USDC.e by:
- Bridging USDC from Ethereum Mainnet using the [Polygon Bridge](https://portal.polygon.technology/bridge)
- Purchasing directly on a CEX that supports Polygon withdrawals (confirm the token is USDC.e / "USDC (PoS)")

**MATIC**

A small amount of MATIC is required to pay Polygon gas fees. A few dollars' worth is sufficient for many hours of operation.

<Tip>
  Start with $10–$20 USDC for initial testing. Use `COPY_DRY_RUN=true` to verify the bot is behaving correctly before committing larger amounts.
</Tip>

## Connect to Polymarket and get your proxy wallet

Polymarket does not let the bot trade directly from your MetaMask address (your EOA). Instead, when you connect your wallet to Polymarket, the protocol deploys a **proxy wallet** — a smart contract that holds your funds and executes trades on your behalf. The bot must be configured with this proxy address, not your MetaMask address.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Connect your wallet to Polymarket">
    Go to [polymarket.com](https://polymarket.com) and click **Connect wallet**. Select MetaMask and approve the connection using the dedicated trading wallet you just created.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Complete onboarding">
    Follow any prompts to approve the proxy wallet deployment. Polymarket will sign a transaction from your EOA to deploy the proxy contract — this costs a small amount of MATIC.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find your proxy wallet address">
    After connecting, open your **account settings** on Polymarket. Your proxy wallet address is shown there. It starts with `0x` and is different from your MetaMask address.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set PROXY_WALLET in your .env">
    Copy the proxy wallet address and set it as `PROXY_WALLET` in your `.env` file. Do not use your MetaMask EOA address here.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  `PROXY_WALLET` must be the Polymarket proxy wallet address — the smart contract address — not your MetaMask EOA address. Using the wrong address will cause order signing to fail.
</Note>

## Get a private RPC endpoint

The bot makes frequent RPC calls to Polygon (order signing, block polling, token approvals). Public endpoints like `https://polygon-rpc.com` are rate-limited and can be slow or unreliable under sustained load, which can cause missed trades or failed transactions.

Use a dedicated private endpoint from one of the following providers — both offer a free tier that is sufficient for personal bot usage:

| Provider | URL | Free tier |
|----------|-----|-----------|
| Alchemy  | [alchemy.com](https://alchemy.com) | 300M compute units/month |
| Infura   | [infura.io](https://infura.io) | 100K requests/day |

After signing up and creating a Polygon Mainnet app, your endpoint URL will look like:

```
https://polygon-mainnet.g.alchemy.com/v2/YOUR_API_KEY
```

Set this as `POLYGON_RPC` in your `.env`.

## CLOB API credentials

<Info>
  Leave `POLY_API_KEY`, `POLY_API_SECRET`, and `POLY_API_PASSPHRASE` blank in your `.env` on first run. The bot derives these credentials automatically from your private key on startup using Polymarket's EIP-712 auth flow and stores them for subsequent runs. Do not fill them in manually unless Polymarket has provided them to you directly.
</Info>
